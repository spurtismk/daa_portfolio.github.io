Introduction

The Design and Analysis of Algorithms course explores how efficient algorithms can solve real-world problems. It includes topics such as recursion, dynamic programming, and graph algorithms, with practical applications in urban planning and infrastructure.

Course Project Overview

This project addresses urban inefficiencies, focusing on traffic flow, waste management, and infrastructure using algorithmic solutions. Team members will tackle various real-world business cases through effective data-driven solutions.

Sarnath City: A Smart City

City Layout

Here is the breakdown of how the land is allocated in the city:

  • Residential Areas: 35-40% (525 - 600 sq km)
  • Commercial and Business Districts: 15-20% (225 - 300 sq km)
  • Industrial Zones: 10-15% (150 - 225 sq km)
  • Green Spaces and Parks: 10-15% (150 - 225 sq km)
  • Transportation and Infrastructure: 10-15% (150 - 225 sq km)
  • Utilities and Energy Infrastructure: 5-10% (75 - 150 sq km)
  • Education and Healthcare: 5-10% (75 - 150 sq km)
  • Cultural and Social Spaces: 5-10% (75 - 150 sq km)
  • Other Miscellaneous Areas: 1-5% (15 - 75 sq km)

Budget

The budget for transforming a 1,500 sq km city into a smart city ranges from $20 billion to $45 billion, depending on factors like technology integration, sustainability, and infrastructure development.

Sustainability

  • Solar-powered street lights
  • Electric roads for transportation
  • Hybrid boats (solar/electric)
  • Hydropower plants
  • Sewage treatment systems
  • 30% green spaces
